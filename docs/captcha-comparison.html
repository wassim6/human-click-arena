⚖️ How the arena compares to the real ones

Where this behavioral detector stands next to reCAPTCHA, Cloudflare Turnstile & hCaptcha — signal by signal — and a concrete roadmap to close the gap.
← Back to dashboard 📊 Signal matrix 🧭 Roadmap GitHub ↗

TL;DR. The arena is a strong, transparent behavioral-shape analyzer with a couple of cheap fingerprint gates and two rate/cost layers. Its movement analysis is genuinely good — conceptually the same sub-signal the commercial systems feed into their models, and it's even ahead on the sub-pixel tell.

The gap isn't mouse math — it's breadth & unspoofability: the big systems score network-layer signals the client can't forge (TLS/JA3-JA4, HTTP/2 fingerprint, IP/ASN reputation), aggregate whole-session + device behavior, and run adversarial ML. Adding the unspoofable server-side tier — not more curves — is what stops a well-humanized stealth browser.

How the commercial systems score

🟦

Google reCAPTCHA

v3 returns a 0–1 risk score per action, no challenge. Mix: browser integrity / headless traits, behavior (mouse, scroll, typing, tab-switch), IP+ASN reputation, history tied to the session/browser/token, and Google cookie trust (a cookied session starts higher). v2 weighs the same before you ever click the box. Enterprise adds reason codes & account-defender risk.

🟧

Cloudflare Turnstile

Non-interactive. Runs background JS micro-challenges — proof-of-work, proof-of-space, browser-API probing (canvas/WebGL/audio/navigator), human signals (mouse timing, focus, jitter) — into distributed ML with adaptive difficulty. Can use Private Access Tokens: hardware attestation that fingerprinting can't fake and stealth can't patch. Rides Cloudflare's network-wide threat intel.

🟨

hCaptcha

Risk score 0–1 from privacy-preserving ML over "thousands of data points": device fingerprinting, behavioral patterns, and intent analysis, with explicit multi-accounting / abuse-at-scale detection. Enterprise scores abuse, not just humanity; falls back to image tasks and runs proof-of-work underneath.

🖱️

human-click-arena

A transparent weighted heuristic over one gesture, one request: movement shape + sub-pixel + the spoofable fingerprint flags + PoW + a crude IP rate limit. Built to be readable and beatable — the opposite of a black box, and the point of the project.

The tier that actually stops the "winners"

A humanized real-browser click on a 1× display is indistinguishable from a human at the event level — the README's honest ceiling. What still separates them lives below JavaScript, where the client can't lie:

TLS fingerprint (JA3/JA4). The ClientHello is set by the TLS stack, not your JS. A stealth browser that claims Chrome but presents a non-Chrome handshake — or pairs a Chrome JA3 with a Firefox UA — is flagged instantly. Proxies hide the IP, not the handshake.

HTTP/2 fingerprint. Frame ordering, SETTINGS, header casing. "Chrome TLS over HTTP/1.1" screams automation tool.

IP / ASN reputation. Datacenter ASN, known proxy/VPN ranges, threat-intel feeds. Residential proxies dodge reputation but still carry the wrong TLS/HTTP fingerprint.

Attestation (PAT). Hardware-backed proof of a genuine device. Stealth can patch a flag; it cannot mint an Apple attestation. None of these need a better mouse model — and none fall to ghost-cursor or playwright-stealth.

🧭 Roadmap to close the gap

Prioritized by impact × unspoofability ÷ effort.

🥇 Tier 1 — unspoofable server-side signals highest leverage

  1. TLS JA3/JA4 + UA-consistency check. Terminate TLS at a proxy that exposes the ClientHello (nginx JA3 module / HAProxy / a small Go front) and pass X-JA4 into Flask. JA3 browser family ≠ UA family → hard fail. A plain Flask/WSGI app can't see the raw ClientHello — this needs a TLS-terminating front end.
  2. HTTP/2 fingerprint + header order/casing captured at the same front end.
  3. IP/ASN reputation. Cheapest immediate win: ship a datacenter-ASN list + proxy/VPN feed; flag non-residential origins. Replaces the raw in-memory counter with a real input.

🥈 Tier 2 — turn the client flags you already collect into real tells

  1. Detect the stealth patch, not the flag. Stealth libs reset navigator.webdriver=false but leave fingerprints: Function.prototype.toString tampering, inconsistent navigator.permissions, missing chrome.runtime, plugin/mimeType inconsistencies. Collect these in collector.js and score them.
  2. Headless detection. WebGL renderer = SwiftShader/llvmpipe, absent AudioContext, screen/orientation & permissions quirks.
  3. Device fingerprint + internal consistency (UA ⇄ platform ⇄ JS-engine ⇄ TLS).

🥉 Tier 3 — broaden behavior & make the scorer a model

  1. Score the whole session, not just the final gesture: scroll dynamics, focus/blur, dwell/hesitation, keystroke timing, multiple gestures.
  2. Replace the linear heuristic with a trained model on the labeled human/bot traces the arena collects — the README's stated end state. Feature extraction stays; only score() changes. Every accepted bypass becomes a training row.
  3. Harden the token: signed, single-use, short-TTL, bound to action + hostname.

🏅 Tier 4 — proof-of-personhood hardest

  1. Private Access Tokens / Privacy Pass attestation. Let genuine devices vouch via OS/hardware attestation — the only tier even a perfect stealth browser can't fake.
What not to invest more in. More mouse-shape math has diminishing returns — the behavioral layer is already near the realistic ceiling for single-gesture analysis, and a replayed real human trace beats any amount of it. Spend the effort below JavaScript (Tier 1) and on breadth (Tier 3).
